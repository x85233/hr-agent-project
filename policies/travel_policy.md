# Acme Horizon Travel Policy

## Purpose
This synthetic policy explains business travel rules and reimbursement expectations for Acme Horizon.

## Scope
This policy applies to employee travel for customer meetings, conferences, office visits, training, and other approved business purposes.

## Eligibility
Full-time and part-time employees may travel when approved for a valid business reason. Contractors may travel only when their contract owner approves and the contract allows reimbursement.

## Booking Requirements
Employees should book travel through Acme Horizon's approved travel platform when available. Economy airfare is standard for domestic flights. Premium cabin airfare requires director approval unless medically required as an accommodation.

## Lodging, Meals, and Transportation
Employees should choose reasonably priced hotels near the business location. Business meals follow the expense policy limits of $25 breakfast, $35 lunch, and $60 dinner. Rental cars require manager approval when rideshare, taxi, or public transportation would not be practical.

## Approval Requirements
Domestic travel requires manager approval before booking. International travel requires director approval, HR review, and Security review before booking. Travel expected to exceed $2,500 requires department head approval.

## International Travel and Remote Work
International business travel does not automatically approve international remote work. Employees who plan to work while abroad must also follow the remote work policy and receive written approval before departure.

## Exceptions
Travel disruptions, safety issues, client emergencies, or accessibility needs may justify exceptions. Employees should notify their manager and document the reason.

## Escalation and Contact
Travel questions should go to travel@acmehorizon.example. Safety or security concerns during travel should be reported to Security immediately.
